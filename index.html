Science Questions

This quiz is a compilation of some questions from the Quiz Show in the SciShow Youtube channel.

In Europe, large alcon blue butterflies can be seen flitting about in the summer, but like many butterfly species, their populations have declined recently due to habitat fragmentation and loss. This particular species is especially sensitive to such changes because it has a kind of particular diet during its last larval stage which relies on the presence of another species. When they're separated from that species, they die because their caterpillars can't get the special food without it. Of course, that species isn't exactly a willing participant in all of this, so the question is, what biological trick do these caterpillars use to get their special meals? Do they:

  1. produce chemicals that get ants to regurgitate their food
  2. sneak around at night so they can eat honeycombs without the bees noticing
  3. look like leaves so they can ambush flies
  4. poop out compounds that lower the defenses of their host plants

In the jungles of Central and South America, communication is key, especially for small social animals like the endangered pied tamarin monkey. Little baby tamarins are especially vulnerable since only the alpha female breeds, the whole group takes care of her babies, so all tamarins react when they hear a baby tamarin in distress, but sometimes, what sounds like the cry of a baby monkey is actually something very different. In 2005, researchers observed something that was definitely not a baby tamarin making the same call near a band of adults. Was it:

  1. a margay cat
  2. adult tamarin from a different troupe
  3. a maned wolf
  4. a harpy eagle

Herons, like a lot of other birds, are super smart, which is part of what makes them such great hunters. For example, they can stand motionless for unbelievable amounts of time, waiting for a fish or frog to make the wrong move, but herons aren't always patient enough to wait for their prey to come within striking distance, and they've developed a special strategy that they can use to catch fish. How do they do it? Do they:

  1. use bait
  2. camouflage themselves with plants
  3. farm the fish themselves in rocky fish ponds
  4. hunt in packs

We humans stand out in a lot of ways, including with our bodies themselves because we have a body part that no other living species has. Which of the following is unique to humans?

  1. having a chin
  2. having heels
  3. having tonsils
  4. having an appendix

Throughout history, people have collected the stuff for a wide variety of things that might sound weird to us, but turn out to actually have some science behind it. Which of the following didn't they use pee for?

  1. topical anesthetic
  2. teeth whitener
  3. hallucinogenic drink
  4. fertility treatment

You might have noticed that plane wings have these little pods sticking out underneath them. They look a little bit like engines but that's not what they are. They are called flap track fairings because they contain the mechanism that moves the flaps on the wings, but they also have another important job. Question is, what else are flap track fairings used for?

  1. reducing drag
  2. reserve fuel tanks
  3. warming up the wings to prevent ice build up
  4. keeping the wings from bending too much

Airplane windows are actually made up of three panes of glass. There's the one on the inside, which is basically a cheap layer that is meant to be the one that gets all scratched up, and then there are two on the outside of the plane that are both built to withstand all the stress that comes with being an airplane window. It's a hard job, but have you ever noticed that there's a little hole at the bottom of the window through the two inner panes of glass? Why is that there?

  1. to equalize the pressure with the inside of the plane
  2. to help firefighters break the window in case of an emergency
  3. to increase the overall strength of the window
  4. to give the window some flexibility as the temperature changes
